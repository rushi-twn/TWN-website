Building one of the top 5 travel brands from scratch through media innovations. A TV show. Stand-up comedy. Theater.

Challenge

C&K was a global travel brand with operations in 12 countries and one of the top three in India. While C&K was very successful as a brand for cosmopolitan audiences, there was a new troubling trend emerging – regionalisation. The brief to the agency was clear, help C&K chart out a complete strategy to get a strong foot-hold in Maharashtra for the next 3 years.

Insight

The focus of this exercise was largely the group product. The target audience for the group product was much older and the average age group was 35-60 years. An attempt to appeal to this audience would mean a complete immersion in Maharashtrian ethos, culture and values, which had implications for both- the product and the communication. We started digging into what appealed to the audience and found out that television habits in these households had changed in the past few years. Regional television had taken the place of national television in prime time and weekend slots. A good deal of time was spent on television viewing, but, instead of taking the conventional ATL route, we saw the opportunity for penetrating this market via content. This led us to the big idea.

Big Idea:

The First International TV show on a Marathi channel!

Detailing

There was no international travel content on Marathi television channels. So the idea was to use a very popular Maharashtrian celebrity and create a travel show. But what should we call it? Inspired by the very popular Marathi suffix to every Marathi surname 'kar', we suggested the brand to be called Amhi Travelkar.

To enhance brand recall further we also named the show Amhi Travelkar. In the show, C&K would make the dream of any traveler, who wanted to explore Europe come alive on television. The most exotic wishes expressed by the viewers would be fulfilled on TV by the celebrity host.
We ran a TV spot and a press campaign to announce this contest. This was supported by on-ground activations and the show went on air in 2012 on STAR Pravah, the leading channel in Marathi.

Results:

Phase 1:

The property began with an ultra-luxurious positioning of being one of Mumbai’s top luxury real estate landmarks. The property was launched in the market through whisper marketing - An exlcusive outreach to the top 1000 CXOs through a tie-up with the Forbes magazine and sponsorship of the Forbes India Leadership Awards. 

The collaterals were built through an art collaboration with some of Mumbai’s top artists. The customer experience at the property was enhanced through augmented reality and state of the art experience zones.

We next went to market with an above the line (ATL) campaign with the commercial inventory targeting high network businessmen for small office spaces. Thanks to the initial build up and a sharper campaign, we managed to sell 50% of the targeted inventory. 

Then the management changed hands, and construction came to a halt. 

Phase 2:

The project was restructured with fresh funding, and Think WhyNot was invited again by the new management to pitch for the renewed marketing efforts. Because of proximity to the project and early involvement, we won the mandate easily and worked on the campaign to launch the commercial and residential towers. A new campaign was designed, new collaterals made, around the positioning of "___". 

Covid struck and everything went into a freeze. However, the silver lining was everyone realised that the only safe place in Covid was their own home. This gave us our new campaign idea - “Home: The Center Of The New Normal”. This campaign was launched just as things opened up.

Phase 3:

Mopping up sales. By now, the property was half-ready. Any lingering negative perceptions about the property were fast getting erased. We launched two campaigns in quick succession. The first one highlighted how Dadar as a neighborhood was getting upgraded thanks to the presence of Kohinoor.

We realised that most of our sales were coming from the neighborhood and hence we activated the neighborhood sentiment through a straight forward testimonial cmapaign. The campaign was titled "Happy To Be Here".
